ECO | 2003 | Economic Principles and Issues | P | C | | A nontechnical introduction to economic concepts such as scarcity, costs and benefits, supply and demand, trade, employment, and growth, with applications to current economic issues and policies. May be applied toward the core curriculum requirement in Social and Behavioral Sciences. (Formerly titled “Introduction to Political Economy.”).

May be applied toward the core curriculum requirement in Social and Behavioral Sciences.
ECO | 2023 | Introductory Microeconomics | P | C | Prerequisite: Placement into a college-level mathematics course. | An introduction to the economic theory of decision making by consumers and business firms; an analysis of the domestic and international market systems and their roles in allocating goods and services; and problems of market failure.
